❤️ Rheumatic Heart Disease (RHD)

Definition

Rheumatic Heart Disease (RHD) is a chronic valvular heart disease that develops as a permanent complication of Acute Rheumatic Fever (ARF). It occurs following an abnormal immune response to untreated Group A β-hemolytic Streptococcal throat infection, resulting in progressive damage to one or more heart valves, especially the mitral valve.

Exam Definition
Rheumatic Heart Disease is permanent valvular damage resulting from one or more episodes of Acute Rheumatic Fever following Streptococcal pharyngitis.

Epidemiology

Feature Description
Causative Organism Group A β-Hemolytic Streptococcus
Age Group 5–15 years
Common Valve Mitral Valve
Developing Countries Higher prevalence because of overcrowding and poor access to healthcare
Preventable? Yes — early diagnosis and treatment of streptococcal sore throat prevents disease.
High Yield
  • Most common acquired heart disease in children in developing countries.
  • Follows untreated streptococcal pharyngitis.
  • Mitral valve is affected most frequently.

Host Factors

Factor Importance
Age Maximum incidence between 5 and 15 years.
Sex Both males and females are affected.
Genetic Susceptibility Some individuals develop exaggerated immune responses.
Socioeconomic Status Poverty, overcrowding and poor sanitation increase risk.
Nutrition Malnutrition predisposes to recurrent infections.

Etiology

The disease is caused by an autoimmune reaction following infection with Group A β-hemolytic Streptococcus. The bacterial infection itself does not damage the heart directly. Instead, antibodies produced against streptococcal antigens cross-react with cardiac tissues, producing inflammation and permanent valve damage.

Pathogenesis

Group A Streptococcal Pharyngitis ↓ Immune Response ↓ Cross-Reactive Antibodies ↓ Inflammation of Heart ↓ Repeated Attacks ↓ Permanent Valve Damage ↓ Rheumatic Heart Disease

Clinical Features

System Features
General Fever, fatigue, malaise, loss of appetite
Cardiac Chest pain, palpitations, breathlessness, murmurs, heart failure
Joints Migratory polyarthritis involving large joints
Skin Erythema marginatum, subcutaneous nodules
Nervous System Sydenham chorea (involuntary movements)
Exam Pearl
  • Mitral regurgitation is the commonest lesion during Acute Rheumatic Fever.
  • Mitral stenosis is the commonest chronic lesion of Rheumatic Heart Disease.
  • Repeated attacks worsen valve damage.

Diagnosis

Diagnosis is based on the Modified Jones Criteria together with evidence of a recent Group A Streptococcal infection.

History of Sore Throat ↓ Evidence of Streptococcal Infection + Jones Criteria ↓ Diagnosis of Acute Rheumatic Fever ↓ Long-term Follow-up ↓ Rheumatic Heart Disease

Evidence of Recent Streptococcal Infection

  • Positive throat culture
  • Rapid antigen test positive
  • Raised ASO (Antistreptolysin O) titre
  • Raised Anti-DNase B titre
  • Recent

    Complications of Rheumatic Heart Disease

    • Mitral stenosis
    • Mitral regurgitation
    • Aortic valve disease
    • Congestive heart failure
    • Atrial fibrillation
    • Stroke due to embolism
    • Infective endocarditis
    • Pulmonary hypertension
    • Sudden cardiac death (rare)

    Prevention and Control

    Health Education ↓ Early Diagnosis of Sore Throat ↓ Complete Antibiotic Treatment ↓ Prevent Acute Rheumatic Fever ↓ Regular Secondary Prophylaxis ↓ Prevent Rheumatic Heart Disease

    Primary Prevention

    Primary prevention aims to prevent the first attack of Acute Rheumatic Fever by early diagnosis and proper treatment of streptococcal sore throat.

    • Early diagnosis of sore throat
    • Throat swab if necessary
    • Complete antibiotic treatment
    • Health education
    • Improve personal hygiene
    • Reduce overcrowding
    • Improve nutrition

    Secondary Prevention

    Secondary prevention prevents recurrent attacks of Acute Rheumatic Fever and progression to severe Rheumatic Heart Disease.

    Duration of Secondary Prophylaxis

    Patient Category Duration
    Acute Rheumatic Fever without Carditis 5 years or until 21 years of age (whichever is longer)
    Carditis but No Residual Valve Disease 10 years or until 21 years of age (whichever is longer)
    Carditis with Residual Valve Disease 10 years or until 40 years of age (whichever is longer); lifelong in severe disease

    Jai Vigyan Mission

    Jai Vigyan Mission is an initiative that promotes early diagnosis, prevention and effective management of Rheumatic Fever and Rheumatic Heart Disease through community awareness, early treatment of streptococcal sore throat, strengthening health services and ensuring secondary prophylaxis.

    • Early diagnosis of sore throat.
    • Prompt antibiotic treatment.
    • Health education.
    • Training of healthcare workers.
    • Regular Benzathine Penicillin prophylaxis.
    • Reduction of Rheumatic Heart Disease burden.
