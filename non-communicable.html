🩺 Epidemiology of Non-Communicable Diseases

Definition

Non-communicable diseases (NCDs) are chronic diseases that are not transmitted from one person to another. They usually have a long duration and progress slowly.

Major NCDs

  • ❤️ Cardiovascular diseases
  • 🩸 Diabetes Mellitus
  • 🫁 Chronic Respiratory Diseases
  • 🎗 Cancer
  • 🧠 Stroke

Risk Factors

  • Smoking
  • Alcohol
  • Physical inactivity
  • Unhealthy diet
  • Obesity
  • Hypertension
  • High blood sugar
  • Stress

Flowchart

Risk Factors ⬇ Obesity / Hypertension / Diabetes ⬇ Target Organ Damage ⬇ Heart Disease • Stroke • Kidney Disease

Mnemonic

"SODA" S → Smoking O → Obesity D → Diabetes A → Alcohol

High Yield

  • NCDs are the leading cause of death worldwide.
  • Most are preventable by lifestyle modification.
  • Early screening reduces complications.
  • Primary prevention is the best strategy.
